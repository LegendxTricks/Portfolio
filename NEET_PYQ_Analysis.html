🎯 HIGH-YIELD PHYSICS TOPICS (Most Repeated Sub-topics)

⚡ MUST-DO Physics Topics

ASKED EVERY SINGLE YEAR

  • Capacitors (parallel plate, series-parallel combinations, energy)
  • Kirchhoff's laws + Wheatstone bridge circuits
  • Bohr's atomic model (energy levels, transitions, spectra)
  • Young's Double Slit Experiment (fringe width, conditions)
  • p-n junction diode (forward/reverse bias, I-V characteristics)
  • Logic Gates (truth tables, universal gates)
  • Nuclear reactions (half-life, decay constant, binding energy)

  • AC circuit numericals (impedance, resonance frequency)
  • Semiconductor: transistor as amplifier / switch
  • EM waves — applications & wavelength identification
  • Statement-based questions on laws of physics
  • Match-the-list questions (2024–25)
  • Graph-based questions (V-T, I-V, P-T graphs)

🌟 BOTANY GOLDEN TOPICS — Asked in 6+ of 8 Years

MOST REPEATED BOTANY CONCEPTS

  • DNA structure: A=T, G≡C; antiparallel, 3'→5' template
  • Operon model (Lac operon — inducible; Trp operon — repressible)
  • Meiosis I: Stages of prophase I (leptotene → diakinesis)
  • Embryo sac: 7-celled, 8-nucleate; central cell = 2 polar nuclei
  • C3 vs C4 plants: Rubisco, bundle sheath cells, hatch-slack pathway
  • Restriction enzymes: palindromic sequences, sticky/blunt ends
  • Mendelian laws: incomplete dominance, codominance, ABO blood group

BOTANY — NCERT DEPENDENCY

  • ~90% questions traceable to NCERT text + diagrams
  • Pay special attention to NCERT tables (e.g., comparison of kingdoms)
  • NCERT examples and exercises are frequently reproduced
  • Bold terms in NCERT = often direct question material
  • Diagrams: embryo sac, lac operon, Calvin cycle — draw & memorise
  • Chapter summaries in NCERT are exam-critical

Common Traps & Negative Marking Pitfalls

PHYSICS TRAPS (Most Common)

  • Work done by magnetic force = 0 (perpendicular always)
  • Efficiency of Carnot engine formula — use absolute temperatures (Kelvin)
  • de Broglie wavelength — using non-relativistic vs relativistic formula
  • Capacitor with dielectric — energy increases/decreases depending on condition (V const. vs Q const.)
  • AC circuit: power factor = 0 for pure L or C circuits
  • Logic gate questions — NAND as universal gate

BIOLOGY TRAPS (Most Common)

  • Antipodal cells = 3 cells (NOT 3 nuclei) — frequently tested trap
  • Double fertilisation: primary endosperm nucleus is triploid (3n)
  • Net ATP from glycolysis = 2 (NOT 8)
  • RQ of carbohydrates = 1; fats less than 1; proteins approx. 0.9
  • Colostrum contains IgA (not IgG) — classic confusion trap
  • Hardy-Weinberg: p² + 2pq + q² = 1 vs p + q = 1 — know which is which

⚛️ Physics Preparation Strategy

MUST-DO CHAPTERS (Top 8 — 70% of Physics)

  • Electrostatics — capacitors, field, potential (highest ROI)
  • Current Electricity — Kirchhoff, Wheatstone, meters
  • Modern Physics — Bohr model, nuclear decay, photoelectric
  • Ray & Wave Optics — lenses, YDSE, polarisation
  • Magnetic Effects + Magnetism — force, torque, solenoid
  • Semiconductors — diodes, transistors, logic gates
  • EMI & AC — Faraday, impedance, power factor
  • Thermodynamics + KTG — Carnot, entropy, rms speed

RESOURCES & APPROACH

  • NCERT XI & XII — must complete fully
  • HC Verma (Concepts of Physics) — selected chapters
  • DC Pandey — practice numericals
  • Focus on formula sheets — Physics is formula-heavy
  • Solve all NCERT examples + exercises
  • For A/R type: conceptually understand each law/principle
  • Avoid spending too much time on difficult numericals (−1 risk)
🧪 Chemistry Preparation Strategy

SECTION-WISE APPROACH

  • Inorganic: Pure NCERT. No shortcuts needed. Read line by line.
  • Physical: Formula + concept + 1–2 examples per formula
  • Organic: Master named reactions + reaction mechanisms
  • Biomolecules & Polymers: NCERT tables are enough
  • Coordination: IUPAC naming + isomers thoroughly
  • Electrochemistry: Nernst equation problems
  • Solutions: Colligative properties — numerical practice

HIGH ROI CHEMISTRY TOPICS FOR 2026

  • Amines — basicity order (asked 6/8 years)
  • Biomolecules — glucose reactions, amino acid structures
  • Coordination compounds — isomers + IUPAC (8/8 years)
  • Equilibrium — pH, buffer calculations (8/8 years)
  • Organic reactions — mechanism of addition/elimination
  • Solid state — unit cells, packing efficiency
  • Aldehydes & Ketones — Cannizzaro, Aldol, named reactions
🌿 Biology Preparation Strategy (Botany + Zoology)

GOLDEN RULE: NCERT IS EVERYTHING

  • Read NCERT Class XI & XII Biology 3–5 times minimum
  • Memorise all NCERT tables, diagrams, and bold text
  • Chapter summaries in NCERT — learn them word-for-word
  • NCERT examples often appear as questions verbatim
  • Focus extra time on: Molecular Biology, Genetics, Ecology, Reproduction
  • Make personal notes for Botany: photosynthesis, cell division
  • For Zoology: endocrine glands, immunity, evolution calculations

HIGHEST ROI BIOLOGY TOPICS 2026

  • Molecular Basis: Lac operon, replication, translation details
  • Biotechnology: PCR, vectors, rDNA technology, CRISPR
  • Human Reproduction: spermatogenesis/oogenesis comparison
  • Inheritance: Mendelian genetics + sex-linked disorders
  • Immunity: innate vs adaptive, antibody classes (IgG, IgE, etc.)
  • Animal Kingdom: phylum characters comparison tables
  • Evolution: Hardy-Weinberg numerical + speciation types

⚠️ Top 10 Mistakes to Avoid in NEET

PREPARATION MISTAKES

  • Ignoring NCERT and relying only on coaching notes
  • Skipping Physics chapters (all chapters have been tested)
  • Not practising assertion-reason type questions
  • Leaving Inorganic Chemistry "for later" (never study it)
  • Not revising older chapters periodically

EXAM-DAY MISTAKES

  • Attempting all 180 Qs without care (−1 penalty is real)
  • Spending too much time on single difficult Physics questions
  • Second-guessing NCERT-based Biology answers
  • Not using Section B wisely (2024–25 pattern)
  • Attempting Organic Chemistry questions without knowing mechanism
