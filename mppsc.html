Mark Distribution Summary (Standardized 2020–2023 Format)

Type 1 — VSA (Very Short)

15 questions × 3 marks (2019–2023)
15 questions × 2 marks (2024)
Answer: 10–20 words / 1 line
All compulsory

Type 2 — SA (Short Answer)

10 questions × 5 marks (2019–2023)
10 questions × 7 marks (2024)
Answer: 50–100 words / 5–6 lines
All compulsory

Type 3 — LA (Long Answer)

5 questions × 11 marks (2019–2023)
5 questions × 10 marks (2024)
Answer: 200 words
Internal option available (except 2024)

⚠️ 2024 FORMAT ALERT — Critical Change

  • VSA marks REDUCED from 3 to 2 per question (saves time but lower marks)
  • SA marks INCREASED from 5 to 7 (more writing needed per SA)
  • NO internal options in 2024 LA — you must answer ALL 5 compulsorily
  • LA reduced from 11 to 10 marks each — but 5 compulsory = 50 marks total
  • SA total = 70 marks in 2024 vs 50 in earlier years — SA is now the MOST important section!

📝 Key Insight on Directive Words

  • "Write a short note on" = Most common format — prepare 2–3 para template for all major topics
  • "Evaluate/Critically examine" — Used for British rule impact, Freedom movement leaders, Mughal policy
  • "Describe/Explain" — Standard for Geography long answers — always include diagram/map reference
  • Statement-based questions ("XYZ — do you agree?") appear 2–3 times per History section
  • 2024 shift: More "explain in 60 words" for 7-mark SAs — requires crisp, dense answers

Structure: Background → Causes → Events → Effects → Legacy
📊 Achievements/Evaluation Format
Use for: Ahilyabai Holkar, Ashoka's Dhamma, Shivaji's administration, Chandela ruler Vidyadhar
Structure: Introduction → Positive contributions → Limitations → Overall assessment
✍️ Statement Analysis Format
Use for: "British rule increased poverty", "Muhammad Tughlaq was mixture of opposites", Tilak extremism
Structure: Statement → Evidence for → Evidence against → Balanced conclusion
📝 Short Note Format (SA type)
Use for: All SA questions — Jhanda Satyagraha, folk movements, MP personalities
Structure: Who/What → When/Where → Significance → Legacy (2–3 para, 5–6 lines)

Geography Answer Formats

🗺️ Diagram/Map-Supported Format
Use for: Drainage systems, physiographic divisions, cyclone diagrams, monsoon flow, Malwa Plateau
Always draw: labelled sketch maps for Indian geography topics
📋 Classification Format
Use for: Soils, rocks, forest types, cyclone types, disaster types, RS platforms
Structure: Definition → Classification table → Features of each → Distribution
⚖️ Comparison Table Format
Use for: Northern vs Southern India drainage, Koppen vs Thornthwaite, tropical vs temperate cyclones
Always use a table for comparison questions — saves time and looks structured
🔧 Process/Elements Format
Use for: Remote sensing process, GIS elements, disaster management steps, GPS working
Structure: Definition → Components → Process flowchart → Applications → Limitations
📍 MP-Specific Regional Format
Use for: All MP geography topics — forests, tribes, minerals, plateaus, rivers
